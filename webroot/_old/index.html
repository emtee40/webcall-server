Soundcheck

WebCall telephony offers very high sound quality. And you don't need to install any software to make use of it. Here you can try it out.

When you click the buttons below, your web browser will initiate real telephony calls. You will hear audio being played back on the other side and streamed over to you. This will sound exactly like WebCall telephony. Because this is exactly what it is.


Making and Receiving WebCalls

To make phone calls on the Web, you only need to open a link. However, in order to receive calls, you do need a phone number. But getting one is quick and easy. You just need to enter a password in the box below.


Click to get a WebCall phone number


After you have entered a password you will receive your personal callee link. Your callee link enables you to receive calls. It looks like this (but with a different number):

https://timur.mobi/callee/592510240

Inside the callee app you will find a second link: your WebCall user link. It looks like this:

https://timur.mobi/user/592510240

Your user link is essentially your phone number. You can share it with anyone. And anyone who has it, will be able to call you.

Why not call yourself to verify everything is working correctly? Just open your user link on a second device and click on the Call button. The callee app on your first device should start ringing immediately. This is what you will see when the call arrives:

New Featurs History

June 2021

New: One-Click Telephony for Websites
You can now add telephony buttons to your website and this way enable your online visitors to give you direct 1-click phone calls. The visitors will stay on your website the whole time.


New: Call Notifications for Offline Callees
When you receive a call while you are not online, we can now notify you via social media. If you enable this feature we will ask your caller to wait in line for a few minutes.

May 2021

New: Post-Call Statistics
When you finish a call you can check how much data was sent in both directions, as well as the number of dropped packets, the roundtrip delay in milliseconds and the overall call duration.
This particular call took about 24 minutes. In this time frame 16 MB were sent and 27 MB were received through the Opus audio codec.
The effective bitrate (bps) also differs between the two sides. Most likely one side has spoken longer than the other side. In both cases the bitrate is much lower than the max bitrate configured in WebCall (240 kbps). When you don't speak, the effective bitrate drops to 20 kbps.

April 2021

New: Digital Answering Machine functionality
This feature is for internal use. It allows us to offer the playback of audio to demonstrate the sound quality of true peer-to-peer telephony.

New: WebCall Duo - Two-Party Quick Connect
This feature allows two parties to establish a telephone connection quickly and without either side revealing their permanent phone number.
