WebCall Core Information

Audiophile Web-Telephony

• Browser-to-browser telephony based on WebRTC 1.0.
• End-to-end encryption all the time.
• Peer-to-peer connectivity whenever possible.
• Does not use actual phone numbers.
• No party needs to install any software.
• Delivers exceptional sound quality based on the Opus codec with up to 240kb/s in each direction (20kb/s when silent)

End-to-end Encryption

The two WebCall clients (caller and callee) create standard WebRTC 1.0 connections. The WebCall signaling server (or rendesvouz server) is assisting the two clients in doing so. If a peer-to-peer connection cannot be established, the signaling server will also host a relayed connection, so calls will always go through. The data connection is encrypted by the two clients alone. There is no way for any other party, including the WebCall signaling server, to "look inside" the actual data stream. You can look at the deployed WebCall client source code and verify these claims:

https://timur.mobi/user/caller.js
https://timur.mobi/callee/callee.js

Peer-to-Peer or Relayed

A WebRTC audio connections between two clients is made up of two fully independent data links. The two browsers will only provide access to a connection when both links have been established, so it looks like one. But under the hood we are dealing with two separate links: your audio traveling to the other side and the other parties audio traveling towards your device.

Both links may fail to establish a peer-to-peer connection. Or one will be p2p, while the other will be relayed. WebCall will let you know what is going on by showing a connect indicator that contains one of the following strings: "p2p/p2p", "p2p/relayed" or "relayed/p2p". The link type on the left side is for the audio traveling from you to the other side.

If a p2p connection cannot be established, the problem is most often caused by NAT devices deployed by the ISP's involved. If you often get relayed connections, you may want to try using WebCall via a different ISP. The disadvantage of a relayed connection is a slightly longer ping duration.

Audiophile Sound Quality

WebCall let's you talk to a person on another continent and it provides exceptional sound quality while doing so.

"When I talk to someone on the phone, I really like to be able to make out nuances in their voice. Communicating becomes much richer this way." -Timur

But if the sound quality is so good, why am I told to wear a headset? The reason is: you don't want to spoil the excellent sound quality with echos and echo cancellation effects.

If you have a 100ms delay from one side to the other (not too much by itself), and the person you talk to does not wear a headset, your voice will exit the speaker and enter the microphone on the other side and it will travel back, arriving in your speaker after 200ms. An echo like this is very annoying, which is why there is echo cancellation in place to prevent it. But echo cancellation never works perfectly. It tends to cut out short periods of audio, which often makes it difficult to fully understand all the words being said.

If both side wear a headset, echos don't occur and echo cancellation will not be activated. Only then will you get the full benefits of audiophile telephony.
